Pulmonary Vein Isolation for Atrial Fibrillation



  Atrial fibrillation (AFib) is the most prevalent sustained cardiac arrhythmia in which the atria quivers rapidly. Fibrillation starts with electrical triggers originating at the pulmonary veins that continuously circulate round the atria.



PLACEHOLDER IMAGE



  To prevent the triggers from reaching the atria, patients can undergo pulmonary vein isolation therapy. This can be achieved by a variety of methods, including most recently, cryoballoon ablation . For this method, a balloon is inflated and pressed against the pulmonary vein opening, after which nitrous oxide is injected into the balloon to cryogenically ablate tissue.


Yet, regardless of the method of ablation, approximately 1/3 patients still experience AF post-therapy. To investigate this, we developed a novel MRI sequence that allows imaging of angiography and scar with the same sequence.
